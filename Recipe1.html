Biriyani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • For Marinating Chicken:
    • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 cup yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • Salt to taste

  • For Rice:
    • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
    • 4-5 cups water for cooking rice
    • 1-2 bay leaves
    • 4-5 whole cloves
    • 4-5 whole green cardamom
    • Salt to taste
    • For Biryani Masala:
      • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
      • 2 tomatoes, chopped
      • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
      • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
      • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
      • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
      • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
      • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
      • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
      • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

      • For Layering:
        • Fried onions (from the biryani masala preparation)
        • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)
        • Ghee for drizzling

Instructions:

Marinate the Chicken:
In a bowl, mix the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour. Cook the Rice:
Boil the soaked basmati rice with bay leaves, whole cloves, whole green cardamom, and salt until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the water and set aside. Prepare Biryani Masala:
In a large pan, heat oil or ghee. Fry the thinly sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until the raw smell disappears.
Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft.
Add cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook the masala until the oil separates. Cook the Marinated Chicken:
Add the marinated chicken to the masala and cook until the chicken is almost cooked through. Layering the Biryani:
In a heavy-bottomed pot or biryani pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice.
Spread the cooked chicken and masala over the rice.
Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves.
Add the remaining rice on top.
Final Layering:
Drizzle saffron-soaked milk (optional) and ghee on top.
Sprinkle the remaining fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.
Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):
Cover the pot with a tight lid or aluminum foil. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes. This process is known as "dum" cooking.
Serve:
Gently mix the layers before serving. Serve hot with raita or your favorite side dish.
Enjoy your delicious homemade chicken biryani! Adjust the spice levels and ingredients to suit your taste.
