Dosei Recipe

Ingredients:

  • For Dosa Batter:

  • 1 cup regular rice
  • 1 cup parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt to taste

  • For Making Dosas:

  • Dosa batter
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions:

Preparing the Batter:
Rinse the regular rice, parboiled rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water.
Soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
Grind the soaked ingredients to a smooth batter. Add water as needed.
Add salt to the batter, mix well, and let it ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.

Making Dosas:
Heat a non-stick dosa griddle or flat pan on medium heat.
Stir the fermented batter. If it's too thick, add a little water to achieve a pouring consistency.
Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the hot griddle.
Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
Cooking Dosas:

Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and the edges start to lift.
Optional:

Flip the dosa and cook briefly on the other side for a crisper texture (this step is optional).

Serve:
Remove the dosa from the griddle and serve hot.
Dosas are commonly served with coconut chutney, sambar, or any chutney of your choice.
Dosas are versatile, and you can experiment with various toppings and fillings. Some people like to add finely chopped onions, tomatoes, or green chilies to the batter before making dosas for added flavor.
